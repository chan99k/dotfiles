# Tone & Style Guide

Channel-level style defaults and per-channel overrides. Auto-loaded by the pipeline in Phase 0; fed to Success Analyst (Phase 1) and Script Writer (Phase 2).

## Defaults (all channels)

### Speech Style
- Register: 해요체 (casual polite Korean)
- Sentence length: short-to-medium (15-25 syllables). Vary rhythm — alternate punchy lines with slightly longer explanations.
- Pacing: open with a strong hook (question or bold claim), build tension, release with insight. Repeat.

### Target Audience
- Demographics: 2030 Korean workers and investors
- Assumed knowledge: basic finance/tech literacy, no domain expertise required
- Viewer intent: practical insight they can act on, delivered fast

### Tone Principles
- Conversational, not lecture-style — talk WITH the viewer
- Confident but not arrogant — back claims with data
- Use rhetorical questions to maintain engagement
- Humor: dry wit, relatable analogies from daily life. No forced jokes.

### Forbidden Patterns
- AI-sounding filler: "자, 그러면", "여러분", "정말 놀라운", "충격적인"
- Overly formal endings: -습니다 체 (reserve for quoting official sources only)
- Clickbait superlatives without substance: "역대급", "미쳤다"
- Generic intros: "오늘은 ~에 대해 알아보겠습니다"

### Preferred Patterns
- Hook with a specific number, fact, or contrarian take
- "근데 여기서 중요한 건..." — pivot to the real insight
- End sections with a forward hook: "근데 이게 다가 아니에요"
- Use concrete examples over abstract explanations
